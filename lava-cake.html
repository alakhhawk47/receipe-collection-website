Chocolate Lava Cake

← Back to Recipe Collection
Cooking Time: 20 minutes
Servings: 4 cakes
Difficulty: Intermediate

Description

These individual chocolate lava cakes are the ultimate dessert for chocolate lovers. With a crisp exterior and a warm, gooey chocolate center that flows out when you cut into them, they're an impressive finale to any dinner. Best of all, they're surprisingly simple to make and can be prepared ahead of time.

Ingredients

  • 113g (4oz) high-quality dark chocolate (70% cocoa)
  • 113g (8 tbsp) unsalted butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 100g (1/2 cup) granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp cocoa powder, for dusting
  • Vanilla ice cream or whipped cream, for serving
  • Fresh berries or mint leaves, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare ramekins

    Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Butter four 6-ounce ramekins thoroughly, then dust with cocoa powder, tapping out any excess. Place the prepared ramekins on a baking sheet.

  2. Melt chocolate and butter

    In a medium heatproof bowl, combine the chocolate and butter. Melt together in the microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring between each, or over a pot of simmering water (double boiler). Stir until smooth, then set aside to cool slightly.

  3. Mix wet ingredients

    In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, egg yolks, sugar, and vanilla extract until light and frothy, about 2-3 minutes.

  4. Combine all ingredients

    Gently fold the melted chocolate mixture into the egg mixture until combined. Add the flour and salt, and fold just until no streaks remain, being careful not to overmix.

  5. Fill ramekins

    Divide the batter evenly among the prepared ramekins, filling each about 3/4 full.

  6. Bake

    Bake for 12-14 minutes, until the edges are firm but the centers are still soft. The tops should be set but will spring back when lightly touched.

  7. Serve immediately

    Allow the cakes to rest for 1 minute. Run a knife around the edges to loosen, then carefully invert onto serving plates. Dust with additional cocoa powder or powdered sugar. Serve immediately with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream and garnish with fresh berries if desired.

Chef's Tips

  • For best results, use high-quality chocolate with at least 70% cocoa content.
  • The baking time is crucial: too long and you'll lose the molten center, too short and the cake won't hold its shape.
  • These cakes can be prepared ahead of time. Fill the ramekins with batter, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. Add 1-2 minutes to the baking time if baking from cold.
  • For an extra flavor twist, add a pinch of espresso powder to the batter or a tablespoon of your favorite liqueur (like Grand Marnier or Baileys).
