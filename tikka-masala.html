Chicken Tikka Masala

← Back to Recipe Collection
Cooking Time: 45 minutes
Servings: 4 people
Difficulty: Intermediate

Description

Chicken Tikka Masala is a beloved Indian-inspired dish featuring marinated chicken chunks in a creamy, aromatic tomato sauce. This dish combines a perfect balance of spices with a rich, velvety sauce that's ideal for soaking up with naan bread or serving over basmati rice. While the origins of this dish are debated, its popularity worldwide is undeniable.

Ingredients

  • For the chicken marinade:
  • 700g (1.5 lbs) boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 1-inch chunks
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • For the sauce:
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 can (400g/14oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Basmati rice or naan bread for serving

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken

    In a large bowl, combine all marinade ingredients. Add chicken pieces and toss to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.

  2. Cook the marinated chicken

    Preheat oven to 450°F (230°C). Thread chicken onto skewers and place on a lined baking sheet. Bake for 15 minutes until slightly charred. Alternatively, cook under a broiler or on a grill. Once cooked, set aside.

  3. Start the sauce

    Heat oil in a large pot or deep skillet over medium heat. Add onions and sauté until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add garlic and ginger, and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  4. Add spices

    Add cumin, garam masala, coriander, and paprika to the pot. Stir and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant, being careful not to burn the spices.

  5. Add tomatoes

    Pour in the crushed tomatoes and tomato paste. Stir well and bring to a simmer. Cook for 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly and the tomatoes break down.

  6. Blend the sauce

    For a smoother sauce, use an immersion blender to puree the sauce, or carefully transfer to a blender, blend until smooth, and return to the pot.

  7. Add cream and chicken

    Reduce heat to low. Stir in the heavy cream, sugar, and salt to taste. Add the cooked chicken pieces (removing from skewers if used) and simmer gently for 8-10 minutes to let the flavors meld.

  8. Serve

    Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with basmati rice or naan bread.

Chef's Tips

  • For the most tender chicken, don't skip the marinating step – overnight is best.
  • If you prefer a milder dish, reduce the amount of chili powder in the marinade.
  • For a dairy-free version, coconut milk can be substituted for the heavy cream.
  • The sauce can be made ahead of time and refrigerated for up to 3 days. Simply reheat and add the cooked chicken before serving.
  • For an extra touch of richness, add a tablespoon of butter to the sauce just before serving.
