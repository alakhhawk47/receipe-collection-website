Pasta Carbonara

← Back to Recipe Collection
Cooking Time: 25 minutes
Servings: 4 people
Difficulty: Intermediate

Description

Pasta Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish from Rome made with eggs, hard cheese, cured pork, and black pepper. The dish took its modern form and name in the mid-20th century. This creamy, savory pasta dish combines simple ingredients into a luxurious meal that's perfect for dinner parties or weeknight comfort food.

Ingredients

  • 350g (12oz) spaghetti or linguine
  • 150g (5oz) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 100g (3.5oz) Pecorino Romano cheese, freshly grated
  • 50g (1.8oz) Parmesan cheese, freshly grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt, for pasta water
  • Extra grated cheese and black pepper for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the sauce base

    In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs, egg yolks, grated Pecorino Romano, and Parmesan cheese. Season with a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper. Set aside.

  2. Cook the pasta

    Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook until al dente according to package instructions, usually 8-10 minutes.

  3. Cook the pancetta

    While the pasta is cooking, heat a large, deep skillet over medium heat. Add the diced pancetta or guanciale and cook until crispy and the fat has rendered, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from heat but keep the pancetta in the pan.

  4. Combine ingredients

    Before draining the pasta, reserve about 1 cup of the pasta cooking water. Drain the pasta and immediately add it to the skillet with the pancetta. Toss quickly to coat the pasta in the rendered fat.

  5. Create the creamy sauce

    Allow the pasta to cool slightly (so it doesn't scramble the eggs), then quickly pour in the egg and cheese mixture, stirring constantly. Add about 1/4 cup of the reserved pasta water to create a creamy sauce. If needed, add more pasta water to reach your desired consistency.

  6. Serve immediately

    Serve immediately, garnished with extra grated cheese and freshly ground black pepper.

Chef's Tips

  • The key to a successful carbonara is working quickly and keeping the pasta moving to prevent the eggs from scrambling.
  • Traditional carbonara does not contain cream – the creaminess comes from the eggs and cheese.
  • Freshly grated cheese works much better than pre-packaged grated cheese for this recipe.
